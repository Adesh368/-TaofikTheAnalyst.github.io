National Instant Criminal Background Check System (1998-2022)

Project Overview

The NICS conducts background checks on people who want to own a firearm or explosive, as required by law. When a person tries to buy a firearm, the seller, known as a Federal Firearms Licensee (FFL), contacts NICS electronically or by phone
The prospective buyer fills out theÂ ATF form, and the FFL relays that information to the NICS. The NICS staff performs a background check on the buyer. That background check verifies the buyer does not have a criminal record or isn't otherwise ineligible to purchase or own a firearm. Since launching in 1998, more than 300 million checks have been done, leading to more than 1.5 million denials

Problem Statement

The goal of NICS background checks is two-fold: ensuring the timely transfer of firearms to eligible buyers and not letting firearms fall into the wrong hands.Also, the aim is to check which state has the highest and least firearms permit and at such can be deemed safe

Key Insights

Analysis shows that despite the raging pandemic in the year 2020,the background check request significantly increase to about 40%.
The highest background check was recorded in the year 2020 with 39M checks in total which also recorded the highest firearm of 2.07M permit granted in the United state
The state with the highest and the least firearm permit are Kentucky and Alaska with 31,465,626 and 25,430 respectively.

Recommendations

Public education campaigns may be of value, as some buyers and sellers might not know about the comprehensive background check policies. Thus, education should include information on the penalties associated with violations.
Firearm transfers should not be allowed to occur before background checks are completed.
Access to firearm should be reduce which could slow down mass shootings to minimal.
The applicant for firearm should be properly scrutinize by asking questions like why he or she requesting for firearm at the particular moments.
